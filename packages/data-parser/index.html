DMR Temperature Requirements

Colorado's Discharge Monitoring Report (DMR) requires specific temperature calculations for wastewater discharge compliance. These calculations ensure that thermal discharge meets environmental protection standards for aquatic ecosystems.

Daily Maximum Temperature (DM)

The highest 2-hour rolling average water temperature measured by continuous recorder during any 24-hour period. This captures peak thermal events that could impact aquatic life.

Maximum Weekly Average Temperature (MWAT)

The largest mathematical mean of multiple, equally spaced, daily temperatures over 7 consecutive days. This provides a sustained temperature impact assessment.
